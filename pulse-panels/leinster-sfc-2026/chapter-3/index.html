Chapter Three

Nothing Between Them

Westmeath v Kildare · Leinster Semi-Final
O'Connor Park, Tullamore · 3 May 2026

Twenty-three points were not enough for Kildare. Two goals were enough for Westmeath. That, in the end, was the semi-final.

It was tight from the first whistle. Kildare made the early running and led by four, only for Westmeath to haul it back and edge in front through a Brían Cooney goal. It was level at the break, and level again with the last kick of normal time, when Alex Beirne kicked the equaliser that forced extra time.

Kildare looked the fresher side and scored first in extra time. But Westmeath had a goal left in them — Brandon Kelly forcing home a rebound — and they pulled clear from there. Ten years on from their last Leinster final, the Lake County were back in one.

Daly, full stretch, to deny Cully. Kildare's clearest sight of a goal — and it stayed out.
Cooney's answer was a goal: past Gill, into the roof of the net, Westmeath four clear. The first sign that points alone might not settle this.
Westmeath carrying the ball straight at Kildare. Neither side could shake the other all afternoon.

Then Kildarecame back.

The last kick of normal time. A ball lobbed in, Beirne first to it, and Kildare were level at 0-19 to 1-16. Extra time.
Into extra time the fresher team — but it was Westmeath who found the goal, and Kildare had no reply.
After Extra Time

It came down to goals, the way these games often do. Westmeath raised two green flags; Kildare, for all twenty-three of their points, raised none. That was the margin in Tullamore — and a glimpse of what was still to come. Ten years on from their last Leinster final, Westmeath were one win from the Delaney Cup. Dublin lay in wait.
