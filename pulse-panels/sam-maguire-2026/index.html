All-Ireland SFC · Quarter-final · 28 Jun 2026

Fourteen Men, the Last Four

Reduced to 14 men inside the opening ten minutes, Louth led Monaghan at the break, fell a point behind in the closing minutes, then kicked the last four points in a row. A first All-Ireland semi-final since 1957.

Louth0-27
FT
Monaghan2-18
HT Louth 0-12 · Monaghan 1-7

Louth came to Croke Park as underdogs again, and seven minutes in they were a man down: Seán Callaghan sent off for a high tackle, referee Seán Hurson reaching straight for the red. Four points adrift and a man short for the hour to come, this looked like ending one way. It did not. Monaghan led 1-7 to 0-7 after Oisín McGorman's goal, but Louth closed the half with four unanswered points, Ciarán Downey's two-pointer the pick, to lead 0-12 to 1-7 at the break.

The platform was the kickout. Monaghan were without Rory Beggan, their injured goalkeeper and kingpin; the under-20 keeper Jamie Mooney deputised, and Louth went after his restarts. Dara McDonnell, man of the match at centre-back, won nine kickouts on his own as Louth took 73% of their own. Louth pushed 0-20 to 1-12 clear early in the second half, but Monaghan came again, Jack McCarron's two-pointers and Stephen O'Hanlon's palmed goal hauling them back in front.

Tied three times in the closing quarter, Louth fell a point behind with three minutes left. Then the bench won it. Tadhg McDonnell levelled on 67 minutes, Ciarán Byrne and Ryan Burns kicked the next two, and Monaghan were left chasing a goal. They won a last-minute free and gave it away. Byrne held the ball under the Hogan Stand as the hooter sounded. Louth are in the last four for the first time since 1957, the year they last won the All-Ireland. They play Mayo in two weeks.

73%

Louth's own kickouts won, a man short. They starved Monaghan's extra man of the ball and hunted a rookie keeper's restarts. The numerical disadvantage never told.

Written Off, Then Through

Dublin had lost to Westmeath and Louth and looked finished. Donegal, the favourites, forced extra time after the hooter. Then the written-off Dubs took the extra period by the throat and would not let go.

Dublin2-26
AET
Donegal2-22
HT Donegal 2-7 · Dublin 1-8

Dublin arrived at Croke Park as afterthoughts. Beaten by Westmeath and Louth in back-to-back championship games and relegated from Division 1, they had come through only by edging Cavan in Round 2B. Donegal were many people's favourites. The favourites looked the part early: first-half goals from Shea Malone, who pounced on an intercepted Dublin kickout, and Peadar Mogan, who finished at the Hill 16 end, sent them in 2-7 to 1-8 ahead at the break, a Con O'Callaghan goal keeping Dublin in touch.

Dublin kept coming. Colm Basquel, making his first start in over a year, kicked 0-7. Paddy Small fired a second Dublin goal in the 67th minute, O'Callaghan the provider, to put the Dubs clear. But Donegal would not die. Three points down, they hauled it back, and after the hooter Michael Murphy stood over a free moved to the edge of the arc and split it for two points. Level, 2-18 each. Extra time.

The score sparked a melee. Referee Martin McNally black-carded Murphy and Jason McGee for Donegal and Theo Clancy and Niall Scully for Dublin, so the extra period began 13 a side. It did not slow Dublin. Substitute Seán Guiden struck four points in a row as the Dubs picked off Gavin Mulreany's kickouts, and though Mulreany answered with a two-point free and Ryan McHugh cut the gap to one, Dublin finished with the last three points. Written off a fortnight ago, they are into the last eight. Donegal, the favourites, are gone.

All-Ireland SFC · Round 2A · 14 Jun 2026

The Last Kick

Armagh led by two when the clock went red. Sam Mulroy had one swing from the edge of the arc. The ball dropped, Rafferty could not hold it, and Inniskeen lost its mind.

Louth2-20
FT
Armagh2-19
HT Louth 0-14 · Armagh 1-9

Armagh made the ideal start. Inside two minutes corner back Gareth Murphy finished low into the bottom corner, and the Ulster champions had the early running in front of a sold-out Páirc Grattan. It was going as expected. Then Louth found their feet, and the second quarter was theirs: they hauled in the Murphy goal and a Rafferty two-point free, with a Mulroy two-pointer, a Mulroy clearance off his own line from Cian McConville, and a Ciarán Downey point that sold dummies to two Armagh men before splitting the posts. Louth went in ahead, 0-14 to 1-9.

The second half brought Armagh back. Oisín Conaty levelled it three minutes after the restart, racing onto a Louth turnover at their own kickout and finishing after his first effort was blocked. A Jarlath Óg Burns two-pointer pushed the Ulster champions clear. Then Dara McDonnell, after good work from Craig Lennon, slid home the goal that hauled Louth back into it, on his way to 1-3 from play. Jarlath Óg Burns and Jason Duffy answered with insurance scores. With seconds left, Armagh led by two. The game was theirs. The clock was gone.

Then Armagh coughed it up. Deep in stoppage time, the 70 minutes up on the clock, a turnover handed Louth one last possession and they worked it to Mulroy, a couple of metres outside the 45. He struck it long and high, going for the two-pointer to draw level. The ball dropped under the bar. Ethan Rafferty rose to claim it and palmed it back over his own line. Green flag. The ground erupted. The first championship meeting between these counties ended with the last kick of the game going in.

All-Ireland SFC · Round 2A · 13 Jun 2026

Felled From Distance

Donegal led by seven and looked safe. Then Cork found the range. Six orange flags later, the 2025 finalists were out.

Cork0-17
FT
Donegal1-13
HT Donegal 1-7 · Cork 0-5

Donegal had this won. Or so it looked. Last year's All-Ireland finalists led by seven points after Conor O'Donnell's goal in the 24th minute, Michael Murphy robbing Tommy Walsh at a short kick-out and squaring for the finish. Oisin Gallen had the home side humming early, and at the break Jim McGuinness's men led 1-7 to 0-5.

But Cork had the two-point arc, and they had Steven Sherlock. He finished with 0-9, four of them two-pointers. The Rebels clawed it back, then struck: Luke Fahy and Tommy Walsh landed long-range two-pointers within seconds of each other to lead for the first time with eight minutes to go. Jason McGee levelled, but Sherlock's fourth two-pointer nudged Cork back in front in the 65th.

Donegal, off-colour all afternoon, had chances to save it and spurned them. Turlough Carr pulled a point back, too little and far too late. When the hooter sounded Cork were a point clear and into the All-Ireland quarter-finals. Donegal, beaten at home, dropped into the all-or-nothing third round.
