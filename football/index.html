Into the last four: Louth's 69-year wait is over

The quarter-finals are done and the last four is set. Louth, down to 14 men for over an hour, beat Monaghan to reach their first All-Ireland semi-final since 1957. Kerry survived a real test from Tyrone, Mayo's young side saw off Cork, and Dublin came from six points down to beat Galway. The semi-finals: Kerry play Dublin, Mayo play Louth, in two weeks at Croke Park.

The story of the weekend

Louth are in the last four for the first time since 1957, the year they last won the All-Ireland. They did it the hard way: down to 14 men after an early red card, level with Monaghan deep into the second half, a point behind with three minutes left, and then four points in a row to win it.

The bench decided it. Substitutes Ciaran Byrne and Tadhg McDonnell kicked 0-6 between them, McDonnell levelling the game on 67 minutes before Byrne and Ryan Burns finished it. Dara McDonnell, man of the match at centre-back, won nine kickouts on his own as Louth took 73% of their own restarts. Monaghan, without the injured Rory Beggan, could not hold on.

Around them, the favourites came through. Kerry, the only provincial champion left, survived Tyrone and Darren McCurry's 0-10 off the bench. Dublin's recovery continued, six points down to Galway before a Con O'Callaghan penalty and a 1-8 to 0-1 finish. Mayo's young side ended Cork's wait, now stretched to 14 years. The semi-finals: Kerry play Dublin, Mayo play Louth.

The other cup

The Tailteann Cup

The second-tier All-Ireland, contested by counties who didn't make the Sam Maguire group stage. Same final venue, different cup. Sixteen counties enter; one comes out with silverware.
1
Group stage. Four groups of four. Top two from each group plus a preliminary play-off route into the knockouts.
2
Knockout rounds. Preliminary quarter-finals, then quarter-finals, semi-finals, final.
3
Croke Park. Final played as the curtain-raiser to the Sam Maguire decider in July.
